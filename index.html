Listening · Part 1
Listen and Draw Lines
Match each name to the correct picture by clicking the name then the picture.
LISTENING 1 Listen and draw lines. There is one example.
Script:
Adult: Look at the picture. Where is Emma? She's the girl with the long hair and a red dress.
Child: Oh yes! Emma is near the tree.

Adult: Now you try. Where is Tom? He's the boy with the blue bag.
Child: Tom is on the bus!

Adult: Good! Now, where is Lucy? She has short hair and she's wearing a yellow hat.
Child: Lucy is near the school door.

Adult: Can you find Dan? He is holding a big book.
Child: Dan is next to the teacher.

Adult: And where is Kim? She has a green bag.
Child: Kim is sitting on the bench.

Listening · Part 2
Write a Name or Number
Listen to the conversation. Write the answer — a name or number — on the line.
LISTENING 2 Listen and write. There are two examples.
Script:
Adult: What is your teacher's name?

Adult: How old are you?
Child: I am seven years old.

Adult: What is your best friend's name?
Child: My best friend's name is Sam. S-A-M.

Adult: How many children are in your class?
Child: There are twenty children in my class.

Adult: What number is your house?
Child: My house number is fourteen.

Adult: What is your cat's name?
